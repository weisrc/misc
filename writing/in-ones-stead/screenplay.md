# In One's Stead

#### INT. OLD BEDROOM - NIGHT - 10 YEARS AGO

We see a piece of writing paper that has the same words written on it over and over again.

The sound of a clock ticking can be heard in the background and is slowly getting louder.

`Close-up on MIA's face`

A young girl named MIA is looking at the piece of paper.

`Close-up on the clock`

The sound of the clock is now deafening and it just hits midnight.

`Frontal mid-body close-up on MIA's mother from MIA's perspective`

MIA's mother is standing behind her. She is holding a clothe-hanger in her shaking hands. Suddenly, Mia's mother raises the clothe-hanger and strikes down. The sound of the clock stops.

`Action match cut to:`

#### INT. BEDROOM - MORNING

`Close-up on BRIAN's face`

BRIAN suddenly wakes up from a nightmare. He is sweating and breathing heavily.

`Close-up on MIA's face`

He looks around the room and sees his girlfriend, MIA, sleeping next to him. The bedsheet is all on his side of the bed.

`Wide shot of the bedroom`

BRIAN slowly gets out of the bed and walks out of the bedroom hitting his shoulder on the door frame.

`Action match cut to:`

#### INT. BATHROOM - MORNING

`Medium shot of BRIAN facing the mirror`

He looks at himself in the mirror and sees his reflection. He looks tired and worn out. He splashes water on his face and takes a deep breath.

#### INT. BEDROOM - MORNING

He goes back to the bedroom and re-adjusts the blanket on his girlfriend. He picks up his phone and sees a text message from his mother.

The text message reads: "Can you see if you received an email from our landlord? He said he sent it to you yesterday" in Chinese.

He puts on his clothes and leaves the bedroom.

BRIAN is not sure about one chinese word, so he Google translates it and finds out it means "email."

#### INT. KITCHEN - MORNING

He opens the fridge and takes a few eggs out. Then cracks the eggs directly into a pan and starts cooking them with max heat.

While it cooks, he opens his laptop and checks his email but can't find any email from the landlord.

BRIAN notices that the eggs are burning and he quickly turns down the heat. He takes the pan off the stove and puts it on the counter.

He places the burnt eggs on a plate and takes a bite. He chews the food and swallows it. He then takes his laptop and puts it in his bag.

MIA wakes up and sees BRIAN eating the burnt eggs. She looks at him with a concerned expression in silence.

MIA seats in front of BRIAN.

> BRIAN: They are crispy. I like it this way.

MIA ignores him and gets off the chair. BRIAN notices that he is going to miss his bus and rushes to the door.

> BRIAN: I will miss my bus if I don't leave now. I will see you later.

#### INT. CONVENIENCE STORE - MORNING

BRIAN enters their family-owned convenience store and sees her mother who works during the night shift.

> BRIAN (in Chinese): I couldn't find any email from the landlord. Perhaps he sent it to you?

> MOTHER (in Chinese): No, he didn't send anything to me. I told him to send it to you because I couldn't understand what he said to me. I said "please send email my son."

> BRIAN (in Chinese): Okay, I'll check again. You can go now. I can handle the store by myself.

BRIAN's mother nods and leaves the store.

BRIAN takes his laptop out of his bag and laptop case. He puts the fabric case on the counter and opens his laptop. He logs in and opens out gmail to search for an email. 

He finds a rejection letter from a job he applied for. He sighs and closes the email.

He finds an email from the landlord in the junk mailbox and opens it. The email reads:

> Hi Brian,
>
> I hope you are well!
>
> The lease for the apartment is up for renewal. I have attached the new lease agreement to this email. Please review it and let me know if you have any questions or concerns.
>
> Let me know of a suitable time for me to come by and inspect the apartment and have you sign the new lease agreement. I will be available on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday from around 4pm to 6pm.
>
> Thanks,
> Bill

BRIAN opens the attached lease agreement and reads it. He notices that the rent has increased by $100. He sighs and puts his laptop away. He starts drafting a reply to the landlord.

#### INT. IMESSAGES - MORNING

Meanwhile, he receives a text message from MIA and continues conversing with her.

> MIA: The kitchen is a mess. This is fourth time already.

> BRIAN: Sorry, I was in a rush this morning. I will clean it up after my shift.

> MIA: I already cleaned it up.

> BRIAN: Thanks. Love you.

> MIA: You will learn to clean up after yourself. I am tired of cleaning up after you.

> BRIAN: I do love you. I was in a rush this morning.

#### INT. CONVENIENCE STORE - MORNING

A blind customer enters the store. This is DANIEL. BRIAN puts his phone on the fabric laptop case and greets the customer.

MIA keeps texting BRIAN while he is handling the blind customer. She is upset and frustrated.

> DANIEL: Hey, Brian. How's it going? Just here to pick up the usual stuff.

> BRIAN: What's up man? I am doing well. How about you?

> DANIEL: I am doing well too. You sound a bit tired for a Monday morning.

> BRIAN: Yeah, I had a nightmare last night. But it's okay, I'm fine now.

The customer goes down the usual aisle to pick up some beef jerky and starts touching the items on the shelf.

> DANIEL: Hmmm, I can't seem to find the beef jerky. Can you help me out?

BRIAN starts walking towards DANIEL oblivious to the text messages from MIA. He is focused on helping the customer.

> BRIAN: Oh, sorry. My mom reorganized the store last night. The beef jerky is now on the other side of the aisle. Hold onto my arm.

> MIA (Message): I just feel like you don't care about me.

> BRIAN: Just follow me.

BRIAN walks with the customer to the other side of the aisle and takes their hand to the item.

> BRIAN: Here you go. This is the beef jerky you are looking for.

> DANIEL: Thanks buddy. I appreciate it.

> BRIAN: No problem. Is this all you need today?

> DANIEL: Yes, that's- Oh, actually, a bit thirsty. Any suggestions for a drink?

> BRIAN: Sure, we have a variety of drinks. We have soda, juice, and water. What do you feel like having?

> DANIEL: I think I'll go with a soda. Can you bring me Coke.

> BRIAN: Sure, its on the way back to the checkout counter. Just follow me.

Now, BRIAN guides the customer to the checkout counter picking up a can of Coke on the way. He puts the can of Coke on the counter and starts scanning the items.

> MIA (Message): At my place, I take care of everything.

After scanning the items, BRIAN tells the customer the total amount.

> BRIAN: That will be $15.50. Card I assume?

> DANIEL: Yes, please.

#### INT. MESSAGES - MORNING

BRIAN reads the text messages from MIA on the lock screen.

> MIA (Message): I cook, clean, and even do your laundry. And I work too.

> MIA (Message): I don't want to go on like this anymore.

BRIAN panicking starts writing a reply to MIA who is also typing, but before he can send his message, MIA sends another message.

> MIA (Message): I am done with you. Take your stuff and leave before I come back from work.

> BRIAN (Message): sorry, I was busy helping a customer.

MIA doesn't reply and there is no read receipt. The sound of the clock ticking can be heard in the background. He starts walking around the store with his hands on his head everytime he sends a message to MIA. He is watching their past video recordings on his phone.

> BRIAN (Message): I will do better. I promise.

`insert MIA BIRTHDAY VLOG`

> BRIAN (Message): Please reply to me.

> BRIAN (Message): Are you okay? I am worried about you.

#### INT. CONVENIENCE STORE - 2PM

He is hungry now. He prepares instant noodles. He is still waiting for MIA to reply.

Suddenly, BRIAN receives a phone call. He was expecting it to be MIA, but it is actually his mother.

> BRIAN (in Chinese): Hey, mom. What's up?

> MOTHER (in Chinese): Did you find the email from the landlord?

> BRIAN (in Chinese): Yes, I found it. I will reply to him after my shift. I have something coming up. She is upset with me. I need to go home and talk to her.

> MOTHER (in Chinese): Okay, I am coming to the store.

> BRIAN (in Chinese): Did you sleep enough?

> MOTHER (in Chinese): I slept for a few hours. I am fine.

#### INT. MIA APARTMENT - MORNING

MIA is dumping BRIAN's belongings into a cardboard box in hurry while attending a morning scrum meeting for work on Zoom on her laptop.

> MANAGER: Now, its Ben's turn.

> BEN: Didn't do much yesterday. I was helping the people from DKBU onboard our new API. Somehow, they are not authorized in our API even after registering their OIDC client with us.

> BEN: I plan to continue working on 3898 this afternoon.

> MANAGER: BU are chasing us for this. We must have it done this release.

> BEN: Will try.

> MANAGER: MIA, I see that you joined!

> MANAGER: MIA?

MIA is in the bathroom putting BRIAN's toothbrush in a Ziploc bag. She faintly hears her manager calling her and walks towards her laptop to unmute herself.

> MIA: Hi! I was having trouble setting up my mic. How was the turnover?

> MANAGER: It didn't go to well. The app keeps crashing on a certain trade.

> MIA: How so? Is it at startup or after some time running?

> MANAGER: It is after a few hours of running. Then it kept crashing. There is this one request on our message queue from upstream systems that is too demanding for our system to handle. Right now, we moved to our error queue. Can you investigate and resolve it? This trade needs to be processed before EOD.

> MIA: Yes, I can investigate. I am heading to office soon.

> MANAGER: Great. Other than that, do you have any updates on your tickets. We need 3954 and 3956 in QA this Wednesday. Can you make it?

Meanwhile, MIA opens BRIAN's drawer and dumps all his clothes inside the cardboard box.

> MIA: I have a blocker on adding QBIL ref data, 3956. The team handling it is prioritizing supporting other business lines first. I think we will need to move this to next release unfortunately. The other ticket is going smoothly. We raise a PR this afternoon.

> MANAGER: Okay, just let our business users know.

> MIA: Of course.

> BEN: I may need some of your time MIA this afternoon since you worked on the OIDC part of our new service. We can talk tomorrow as well if you are too busy today.

> MIA: I can try today during the afternoon.

> MANAGER: Great. Joe?

Joe doesn't respond.

> MANAGER: Joe, are you here. If you are, we don't hear you.

> MANAGER: Maybe he will be back soon. Elene?

After packing everything, she puts the box on the floor next to the entrance. She then takes a piece of paper and writes a note to BRIAN. While, she is writing the note, she receives the text messages from BRIAN and ignores them.

The meeting continues on as MIA heads out of the apartment.

> MANAGER: Nick

> NICK: Hey! I have raised the PR for 3959 yesterday. Still waiting for one more reviewer.

> MANAGER: I will review it today.

> NICK: Thanks. Will work on 3962 this afternoon.

> MANAGER: Thanks.

#### INT. CONVENIENCE STORE - 3PM

Her mother arrives at the store and sees BRIAN. She is concerned about him.

> MOTHER (in Chinese): Go, go home and talk to her. I will handle the store.

BRIAN nods and leaves the store.
